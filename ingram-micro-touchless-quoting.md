**Role:** Product Manager · Ingram Micro
**Timeline:** Jun 2024 – Present
**Scope:** EMEA & APAC · $5B+ portfolio · World's largest technology distributor

---

## 🧩 Background

Ingram Micro connects global technology vendors (Lenovo, Dell, Cisco) with resellers and wholesalers (Croma, Bechtle, Reliance Digital) across 50+ countries. My role was to lead **vendor onboarding and auto-quoting (AQ) platform initiatives** across Europe and Asia Pacific — transforming how quotes are created, structuted, priced and delivered to resellers and partners.

---

## 🔴 Problem

The deal-to-quote workflow was highly manual and fragile:

- Sales and finance teams spent significant time creating, updating, and validating quotes
- Country-specific rules, vendor variations, local taxes, and currencies made every quote different
- SKU generation, margin verification, exception handling, and pricing checks were all done manually
- Data was siloed across legacy systems with no single source of truth
- As volumes scaled, the process couldn't keep up — errors increased, and quote rework was constant

> **In short:** A $5B+ quoting operation was running on manual effort, spreadsheets, and tribal knowledge.

## 🛠️ My Approach

### 1. Deep Discovery First
I started by mapping where manual effort and failures occurred most frequently — by volume, error rate, and business impact. This shaped the prioritized roadmap.

### 2. Core Automation Built
- **SKU automation:** Digital SKU creation triggered automatically on quote initiation
- **Pricing rules engine:** Automated margin calculations, VAT variations, discount rules, and local currency handling per country
- **Quote lifecycle automation:** Auto-closure of expired quotes, auto-revision when vendor deals changed — eliminating manual rework
- **AI-powered document parsing:** Multi-language document parsing and field mapping to reduce error rate to 5% and cut rework by 60%

### 3. Governance & Control Layer
- Introduced reseller blacklisting and configurable thresholds per reseller
- Added guardrails for unusually high/low margins
- Built targeted alerts for: missing customer info, pricing API failures, currency mismatches, multi-reseller deal complexities

### 4. Adoption & Self-Serve
- Enabled self-serve vendor onboarding with in-app error messaging
- Created onboarding guides and platform playbooks to reduce dependence on support teams
- Led data migration from legacy platform to **Google Cloud architecture** — establishing a single source of truth

### 5. Stakeholder Management Across Regions
Every country team had conflicting needs. I addressed this by:
- Engaging each team individually to understand pain points, urgency, and business impact
- Building a clear, prioritized roadmap balancing global consistency with local requirements
- Proposing interim workarounds for issues that couldn't be resolved immediately
- This approach reduced escalations and built trust across EMEA and APAC

## 💡 Key Learnings

- **Global products need local empathy** — the same feature means different things in Germany vs. Australia. Spending time understanding local context before building saved significant rework.
- **Adoption is a product problem, not a change management problem** — building self-serve onboarding and in-app guidance directly into the platform drove organic adoption without needing a separate training program.
- **Governance enables scale** — adding guardrails, alerts, and thresholds wasn't overhead; it was what allowed the business to trust the system enough to increase automation.
